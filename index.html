In my extended searches, I've found:

In my process, I've found a higher percentage of european users using transit applications with IoT compatibility

Of those cities, those whose transit authority relied on major public transport hosted a high population of students.
In this, timeliness and delay notifications were found to be most important
So, there’s a prudent conclusion: Students contribute to the highest population of transit app users. They care most about important notifications, such as delays and intelligent mapping, and offline maps to save data.

Information Architecture/UX
Information presented in the orders of
Saved route
Next effective
Next effective
Least effective

Relying on the indeterminate results in further delay for users, magnifying fear


In providing an accurate disposition and user experience for riders, I've implemented attentive care into developing an application with empathy.
Busybus is not your standard transit app, this is an application built on knowing your neighbors, and watering their plants when they're away. In this app, your users will remain privy to updates, delays and modifications to existing routes. When a bus is under maintenance, they won't be finding out at six in the morning, five minutes after your bus, but hours ahead of commute.
Notifying users of delays, maintenance and tardiness are a part of a rider's success, and not their downfall.
When a bus, direction or particular line is down, the user will be notified of the two next-best routes. One, which is closer to the user, and second, one that is faster. This can be adjusted, and the user is not forced to automatically pick either/or. These routes can be selected later, as a part of a section under Interruptions, as a new route which can be added to or replace a journey.
